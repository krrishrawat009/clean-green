Activity 1: Solar Electrification of Dhundi Village, Gujarat

Problem Statement

Location: Dhundi Village, Banaskantha District, Gujarat

Current Situation: Dhundi village faces severe electricity shortage with power available only 4-6 hours daily. Frequent blackouts disrupt agricultural water pumping, children's education after sunset, and local businesses.

Key Challenges:

  • 850 households without reliable electricity
  • Agricultural productivity down 40% due to irregular irrigation
  • Students study under kerosene lamps causing health issues
  • Health centers unable to refrigerate medicines
  • Village receives 300+ sunny days annually but no infrastructure to harness solar energy

Households Affected

300+

40%

Proposed Solution: Community Solar Microgrid

System Design: Install 500 kW solar capacity distributed across village buildings with 1 MWh battery storage for 24/7 power supply.

Components:

  • Panchayat building: 100 kW rooftop solar
  • School rooftops: 80 kW solar panels
  • Community center: 60 kW installation
  • Household rooftops: 260 kW distributed solar
  • Central battery storage: 1 MWh Lithium-ion system

System Architecture

Solar Radiation (5.5 kWh/m²/day in Gujarat)
↓
500 kW Solar Panels (Village Buildings & Rooftops)
↓
DC Electricity Generation (2,200 kWh/day)
↓
Central Inverter Station (DC to AC Conversion)
↓
Battery Storage System (1 MWh - 8hr backup)
↓
Village Distribution Network
↓
850 Households + Farms + School + Health Center

Implementation Details

Economic Analysis:

  • Total Project Cost: ₹3.5 crore
  • Central Govt Subsidy (PM-KUSUM): 30% = ₹1.05 crore
  • Gujarat State Subsidy: 20% = ₹70 lakh
  • Net Cost: ₹1.75 crore
  • Village Contribution: ₹2,000/household + microfinancing
  • Annual Savings: ₹45 lakh (diesel + grid electricity)
  • Payback Period: 6-7 years

Execution Timeline:

  • Phase 1 (Months 1-2): Site survey, load assessment, community training workshops
  • Phase 2 (Months 3-5): Solar panel installation on rooftops, inverter setup, battery storage deployment
  • Phase 3 (Month 6): Grid connection, system commissioning, safety training for 15 local youth

Expected Outcomes:

  • 24/7 electricity access for all 850 households
  • 60% reduction in household energy costs
  • 50% increase in agricultural productivity with reliable pump operation
  • Improved education: evening study facilities for 450+ students
  • Better healthcare: medicine refrigeration, medical equipment operation
  • Carbon emission reduction: 550 tons CO₂/year
  • Employment: 15 local technicians trained and employed for maintenance
  • Women's empowerment: evening income generation activities

Reference & Success Story

Inspiration: This project is modeled after Modhera Village in Mehsana district, Gujarat - India's first 24×7 solar-powered village inaugurated in 2022. Modhera successfully installed 1 MW solar capacity serving 1,300+ households with remarkable success.

Key References:

  • Gujarat Energy Development Agency (GEDA) - Solar Rooftop Program
  • PM-KUSUM (Pradhan Mantri Kisan Urja Suraksha evam Utthaan Mahabhiyan) Scheme
  • Modhera Solar Village Case Study - Ministry of New and Renewable Energy
  • Government of India - National Solar Mission Documentation

Modhera Results: 100% renewable energy coverage, ₹60 lakh annual savings, zero electricity bills, complete economic transformation of the village demonstrating scalability of community solar microgrids.

Activity 2: Wind Farm for Coastal Industries, Jamnagar

Problem Statement

Location: Industrial Belt near Jamnagar, Saurashtra Coast, Gujarat

Current Situation: The Jamnagar industrial corridor houses petrochemical plants, refineries, and manufacturing units consuming over 2,500 MW daily, relying heavily on coal-based thermal power.

Key Challenges:

  • Severe air pollution: PM2.5 levels 3× WHO limits
  • CO₂ emissions exceeding 8 million tons annually
  • 15-20% annual energy cost escalation
  • Grid instability during peak demand
  • 500,000+ residents affected by environmental degradation
  • Coastal region with consistent 7-9 m/s wind speeds (untapped resource)

Proposed Solution: 250 MW Coastal Wind Energy Park

System Design: Deploy 75 wind turbine generators (WTGs) rated 3.3 MW each across 3,200 hectares of coastal land.

Technical Specifications:

  • Turbine Model: 3.3 MW capacity, 140m rotor diameter
  • Hub Height: 120 meters (optimized for coastal winds)
  • Total Capacity: 250 MW (75 turbines)
  • Capacity Utilization: 28-32%
  • Annual Generation: 700 million units
  • Advanced SCADA system for real-time monitoring

System Architecture

Power Electronics & Grid Integration System

↓
Industrial Distribution Grid (Refineries & Factories)

Implementation Details

Economic Analysis:

  • Total Investment: ₹1,875 crore (₹7.5 crore/MW)
  • Power Purchase Agreement (PPA): ₹4.20/unit
  • Current Grid Tariff: ₹5.80/unit
  • Annual Savings for Industries: ₹280 crore
  • Project IRR: 14.2%
  • Payback Period: 7.5 years
  • Carbon Credits Revenue: ₹45 crore/year
  • Accelerated depreciation benefits under Income Tax Act Section 32

Execution Timeline:

  • Phase 1 (Months 1-6): Wind resource assessment, land acquisition, environmental clearances, grid connectivity approval
  • Phase 2 (Months 7-18): Civil foundation work, turbine manufacturing and transport, electrical infrastructure development
  • Phase 3 (Months 19-24): Turbine erection, commissioning, grid synchronization, workforce training programs

Expected Outcomes:

  • Displace 550,000 tons coal consumption annually
  • Reduce CO₂ emissions by 1.4 million tons/year
  • Improve Air Quality Index by 25-30%
  • Clean power equivalent to 180,000 households
  • Create 200 direct jobs and 800 indirect jobs
  • Reduce industrial energy costs by 28%
  • Position Gujarat as renewable energy leader
  • Contribute to India's 500 GW renewable energy target by 2030

Reference & Success Story

Inspiration: This project draws from successful implementations like Suzlon's Soda Sanand Wind Park (152 MW) and Adani's Mundra Wind Farm (126 MW) in Gujarat, which demonstrate commercial viability of coastal wind energy with 30%+ capacity factors.

Activity 3: Micro-Hydro for Tribal Villages, The Dangs

Problem Statement

Location: Remote Tribal Villages along Purna River, The Dangs District, Gujarat

Current Situation: The Dangs, Gujarat's only tribal-majority district, has 18 remote villages with zero electricity access affecting 12,000+ tribal residents.

Key Challenges:

  • No electricity access for 12,000 tribal people
  • Villages are 40+ km from nearest grid (₹85 lakh/km transmission cost)
  • Children's literacy rate 40% below state average
  • Maternal mortality 2× state average due to inadequate healthcare
  • Monthly household income ₹4,000 with ₹2,500 spent on kerosene
  • Forest produce deteriorating without processing facilities
  • Health hazards from indoor kerosene smoke
  • Perennial Purna River with 35m natural gradient flows through villages (untapped)

People Without Power

Proposed Solution: 800 kW Run-of-River Micro-Hydro System

System Design: Run-of-river scheme with minimal environmental impact - no large dam, maintaining ecological flow.

Technical Specifications:

  • Water Flow: 2.5 m³/s diverted through weir
  • Gross Head: 35 meters (natural gradient)
  • Penstock: 850m HDPE pipeline
  • Turbine: Crossflow type (handles variable flow)
  • Peak Generation: 800 kW (monsoon), 400 kW (dry season)
  • Annual Output: 4.2 million units
  • Distribution: 11 kV mini-grid to 18 villages (15 km radius)

System Architecture

Purna River (Flow: 2.5 m³/s, Head: 35m)
↓

↓
850m Penstock Pipeline (HDPE) - Potential to Kinetic Energy
↓
Crossflow Turbine (800 kW rated, 60-100% efficiency)
↓
Synchronous Generator (400V, 3-phase AC)
↓
Control Panel & Distribution Transformer
↓
11 kV Mini-Grid Network to 18 Villages
↓
2,400 Tribal Households (230V/400V)

Implementation Details

Economic Analysis:

  • Total Project Cost: ₹12 crore
  • MNRE Grant (Off-grid Program): 60% = ₹7.2 crore
  • Gujarat Tribal Development Fund: 30% = ₹3.6 crore
  • Community Contribution (MGNREGA labor): 10% = ₹1.2 crore
  • Zero Fuel Cost (renewable energy)
  • O&M Cost: ₹25 lakh/year (tribal cooperative managed)
  • Electricity Tariff: ₹3/unit (subsidized for tribal welfare)
  • Annual Revenue: ₹1.26 crore
  • Surplus for Village Development Fund after O&M

Execution Timeline:

  • Phase 1 (Months 1-4): Hydrological survey, tribal community consent (Gram Sabha approval), forest clearance, design finalization
  • Phase 2 (Months 5-12): Civil works including weir construction, powerhouse building, penstock laying using MGNREGA tribal labor
  • Phase 3 (Months 13-18): Electro-mechanical equipment installation, mini-grid distribution network, household connections
  • Phase 4 (Months 19-20): Commissioning, operator training (15 tribal youth certified), community awareness programs

Expected Outcomes:

  • Electricity access to 2,400 tribal households (12,000 people)
  • Educational transformation: digital classrooms, evening study centers
  • Healthcare improvement: vaccine refrigeration, telemedicine facility
  • Economic empowerment: forest produce processing units (tamarind, honey, bamboo)
  • Household income increase by 60% through value-added products
  • Replace 48,000 liters annual kerosene consumption
  • Savings of ₹72 lakh/year, preventing respiratory diseases
  • Women's empowerment: reduced drudgery, livelihood opportunities
  • 15 permanent jobs for tribal youth as technicians
  • Zero carbon emissions vs diesel gensets alternative
  • River ecology preservation: fish-friendly turbine, maintained ecological flow

Reference & Success Story

Inspiration: Similar micro-hydro projects successfully implemented in Uttarakhand's tribal villages (Aarapad, 150 kW) and Himachal Pradesh (Chamera tributary, 500 kW) demonstrate remarkable socio-economic transformation through decentralized hydroelectric power. These projects achieved 98% household coverage with community-managed operations.
